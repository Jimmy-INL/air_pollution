FairAir

A tool for optimal air pollution sensor placement

Picture Credit: Photo by Ren Ren on Unsplash

Background

4.2 million people die each year due to outdoor air pollution.

Government sensors are intentionally placed far away from pollution sources and don't shine light on our most heavily polluted neighborhoods. Low-cost air pollution sensors, such as those manufactured by Purple Air, have a chance to change this. Purple Air sensor count has increased eight-fold in just three years, but sensors tend to be installed in affluent neighborhoods far from polluters, while our neighbors suffering from the most pollution can least afford to install them. We propose a tool for identifying optimal sensor placement to extract the maximum potential out of this sensor explosion.

With an optimal placement tool, activists can install sensors in areas of greatest need, allowing for more accurate pollution estimates throughout a city than through virtual sensing methods alone. We believe that the increase in transparency around air pollution will spur better policy, saving lives and improving the health of our communities. We can save hundreds of thousands of dollars in hardware costs by diverting needless installations in areas already teeming with sensors toward sparser areas where even a few sensors provide much needed information. Finally, we are proud that we will disproportionally benefit disadvantaged communities who cannot afford to purchase these sensors and whose pollution problems would otherwise remain invisible and forgotten.

Approach

Audience

We have spoken with activists, professors, and EPA specialists, and have gone into the trenches on toxic tours and climate marches. The vocation of activism resonated with us the most and we decided to help those who fight for our clean air every day.

Objective

After the passage of California law XXX, which provides funding for installation of sensors in underrepresented areas, what activists have told us is that they need help placing sensors in the best way possible. They are interested in equity, impact, and coverage, so we have created several metrics in our demo and placed the choice in activists' hands.

Differentiator

There are a variety of virtual sensing solutions on the market. They are able to infer to varying degrees of accuracy pollution levels at locations without sensors. This is an important component of the story, but we differentiate ourselves by taking the next step and a comprehensive solution, making recommendations for additional sensors based on a virtual sensing system.
